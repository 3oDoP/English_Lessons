The Use of Animated Films in Teaching Listening in English Lessons

Video 1: The Present

“The Present” is a touching short animated film that tells the story of a boy who initially rejects a puppy given to him as a gift because the dog has only three legs. Over time, the boy overcomes his prejudices and embraces the dog, revealing that he himself uses a prosthetic leg. The film teaches important lessons about acceptance, resilience, and kindness. The video contains minimal dialogue, making it ideal for focusing on visual comprehension and emotional vocabulary.

Short Answer Questions:

  • 1. Why did the boy reject the puppy at first?
  • 2. What made the boy change his attitude towards the puppy?
  • 3. How do the boy and the puppy help each other?

True/False:

  • 1. The boy was immediately happy to receive the puppy.
  • 2. The puppy had a missing eye.
  • 3. The boy and the puppy both had physical disabilities.
  • 4. The boy never accepted the puppy.

Video 2: Hair Love

A short film about a father who learns how to do his daughter’s hair for the first time. Despite facing challenges, he works hard to get it right and make her feel beautiful. The film showcases the bond between a father and his daughter, emphasizing patience, love, and family support.

True/False :

  • The elderly woman believes the boy is stealing her cookies.
  • The boy confronts the woman about the misunderstanding.
  • The woman later realizes the cookies were hers all along.
  • The story ends with a humorous twist.

Video 3: Snack Attack

A short film about an elderly woman who thinks a young man is stealing her cookies at a train station. However, the situation turns out to be completely different, with a surprising and humorous twist at the end. The film highlights how assumptions can lead to misunderstandings and emphasizes the importance of looking at things from a different perspective.

True/False:

  • The boy is the only one who knows how to conquer the stars.
  • The boy is not sure about his role in the family tradition.
  • Father and grandfather help the boy find his own unique way of sweeping the stars.
  • The boy decides to sweep the stars differently from his family.
